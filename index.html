RageDance

Pulse, presence, and non-aggressive movement practice.

RageDance is not a fighting system. It is a way of listening, reclaiming space, and moving with heat without causing harm.

Overview

RageDance is a movement practice for working with strong emotion, stress, and stored tension through pulse-guided, non-aggressive forms. It draws from martial movement, dance, and somatic awareness, but its purpose is not to intimidate or dominate. The aim is to reclaim presence in the body while keeping others safe.

This site is a working notebook: an evolving record of forms, ethics, and simple practices that anyone can adapt. You are always invited to listen to your own body first.

Lineage Movements

These are foundational forms – the roots of RageDance. They are offered as starting points, not commandments. Each movement can be practiced slowly, with breath, and adapted to different bodies.

  • Skyward Arc – a rising, breath-guided arc that opens the right side of the body and declares presence without threat.

Practice Surfaces: The Jute Grid

RageDance can be practiced on almost any surface, but a jute mat with clearly woven “cells” (for example: a jute carpet laid over foam pads) is especially helpful for learning. The woven pattern naturally creates a loose grid.

Why a grid can help

  • It makes angles, steps, and distances easier to describe and remember.
  • It supports safer practice by showing where feet and weight can land.
  • It allows shared language: “step from cell 2 to 4, then pivot toward 7.”

Tool, not rule

The jute grid is a scaffold, not a cage. It exists to help you notice where you are, not to lock you into perfect geometry. RageDance begins in what you feel, not in what the map says. You are always free to step off the lines.

Practice Cards

Practice cards are short, portable invitations to move. Each card holds one movement or pulse cue that can be used on its own – at home, at a bus stop, on a break, or before sleep.
