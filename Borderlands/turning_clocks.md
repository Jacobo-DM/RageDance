# Turning Clocks  
*Embodied Pulse Practice for RageDance*

Turning Clocks is not a formal kata, display, or orientation exercise.

It is the **way RageDance forms are practiced**.

All RageDance patterns, circuits, and sequences are entered
through Turning Clocks â€” as embodied learning rather than performance.

---

## What Turning Clocks Is
Turning Clocks describes a mode of practice in which:

- the body follows momentum rather than plan
- the center is intentionally unsettled
- orientation is explored, not fixed
- awareness is trained through motion

Forms are not demonstrated.
They are *inhabited*.

---

## Pulse Before Shape
In Turning Clocks, the practitioner listens for pulse:

- internal pulse (breath, balance, fatigue, emotion)
- external pulse (sound, light, wind, temperature, movement around you)

The body responds in real time.

A noisy neighbor.
Light entering through branches.
Wind shifting.
The moon appearing and disappearing behind clouds.

All of it is part of the practice.

---

## Orientation as Experience
There is no required order of turning.

If one orientation feels alive, stay there.
If another calls, go there.
If the body wants to linger, let it linger.
If it wants to spiral away, let it spiral away.

The question is not *where am I facing?*
The question is *how does this orientation change my experience?*

---

## Story, Not Display
Turning Clocks can be understood as a **story in motion**:

- what is happening around you
- what is happening through you
- what is happening within you

The body responds as the story unfolds.

There is no audience.
There is no performance.
There is only participation.

---

## Spontaneity & Play
Disrupting center allows for:

- spontaneity
- play
- surprise
- discovery

This is not chaos.
It is responsive order.

Play is not decoration.
Play is the engine.

---

## Awareness Through Disruption
By turning, reorienting, and losing center slightly, the practitioner learns:

- when balance is present
- when precision degrades
- when to pause, breathe, and recalibrate

This trains sensitivity rather than control.

---

## Teaching Notes
- Do not teach Turning Clocks as choreography
- Do not name directions unless necessary
- Encourage responsiveness over correctness
- Normalize stillness, laughter, hesitation, and return

Turning Clocks should feel alive, not rehearsed.

---

## Borderlands Note
Turning Clocks sits at the border between:
movement and story,
body and environment,
self and world.

It is not an add-on to RageDance.

It is how RageDance listens.
