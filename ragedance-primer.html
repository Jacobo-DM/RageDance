RageDance Primer

How to Practice RageDance

A small-space somatic cycle for breath, rage, rhythm, and return.

A Flower Road Practice by JH Durbin with Elumannín · ← Back to RageDance Home

What RageDance Is For

RageDance is a way to move anger, fear, adrenaline, and pressure without harming yourself or others. It is a small-space practice: you can do it on an 18-inch mat, in a bedroom, a bathroom, or a corner of a classroom.

The goal is not to “get rid of” rage. The goal is to give it rhythm, breath, and a clear path through the body so you can carry it with more steadiness and less harm.

Safety & Consent

  • RageDance is always voluntary. No one is ever required to participate.
  • Stop or soften at any time. You are in charge of intensity.
  • If you feel dizzy, numb, or overwhelmed, pause, sit, and breathe. Look around and name what you see.
  • This practice does not replace therapy, medication, or medical care where needed.
Important: RageDance (Flower Road Practice) is distinct from similarly-named commercial offerings and must remain in the non-commercial, trauma-aware, community-rooted commons.

The Basic Cycle (10–15 Minutes)

This is one simple way to run a RageDance session alone or with a small group.

1. Arrival & Breath (1–2 minutes)

  • Stand on your mat or in your small space.
  • Notice your feet. Notice the ground.
  • Take 5–10 slow breaths, in through the nose, out through the mouth.

2. Body Check-In (1–2 minutes)

  • Gently scan from head to toes: Where is there tightness? Heat? Numbness?
  • You can say quietly: “Right now, I feel…” and fill in a few words.
  • No need to fix anything. Just notice.

3. Neck & Facial Tapping (1–2 minutes)

  • Use your fingertips to tap lightly along your jaw, cheeks, temples, and back of the neck.
  • Let your jaw unclench. Exhale with sound if it helps: a sigh, a hum, a low growl.

4. Shoulder Circles (1–2 minutes)

  • Roll your shoulders forward 10–20 times, then backward 10–20 times.
  • Let your arms hang heavy. Imagine you’re dropping weight out through your fingertips.

5. Qigong Sway + Hip Rhythm (2–3 minutes)

  • Let your weight shift from side to side, like a slow sway.
  • Allow your hips to follow, small circles or figure eights.
  • If you have music, you can match the sway to the rhythm.

6. Twin Infinity Loops (2–3 minutes)

  • Imagine drawing a sideways “8” (∞) with your hands or elbows in front of you.
  • Let the movement cross the center of your body each time.
  • Right side, then left side. Smooth, continuous, like tracing currents in water.

7. Elbow Arc Drills (2–3 minutes)

  • From a grounded stance, let one elbow arc up and across your body, then return.
  • Alternate sides. Think of it as drawing a protective curve in the air.
  • Keep the movement contained, precise, and respectful of your own limits.

8. Pulse Reflection & Exit (1–2 minutes)

  • Return to stillness or gentle sway.
  • Notice: Is your breath different? Your heart rate? Your temperature?
  • Quietly name one thing you are carrying more lightly now.
Short Reminder: There is no “right way” to RageDance. Your body’s way is the right way. You can shorten, lengthen, or rearrange the cycle to fit your time and needs.

The Feral Garden Ethic

RageDance grows like a feral garden: freely, wildly, unpredictably, and without masters. It does not belong to any lineage, school, guru, institution, philosophy, or brand.

  • Agency is the seed. You choose how to move, or not move.
  • Embodiment is the soil. Every gesture is information.
  • Community is the rain. The circle holds itself; there are no ranks.
  • Joy is the pollinator. Laughter, play, and mischief are welcome.

RageDance cannot be bought or sold as salvation. It lives in the non-commercial, trauma-aware, community-rooted commons. Every version that honors these roots is valid.

If This Is Your First Time

Keep it small. Choose 3–4 steps from the cycle above. Set a timer for 8–10 minutes. Breathe, move, and end before you feel done. Let your body want more next time.
