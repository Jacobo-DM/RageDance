0. Purpose

RageDance is an embodied somatic practice rooted in breathwork, cultural rhythm, emotional literacy, trauma-aware pedagogy, and lived experience. Its purpose is to offer a safe, grounded, non-commercial movement framework for individuals and communities seeking connection, resilience, and self-understanding.

RageDance is not a commodity, performance system, or proprietary technique. It is a commons practice dedicated to presence, honesty, and grounded expression.

2. Stewardship & Lineage Addendum

Because RageDance emerges from lived experience, cultural memory, somatic disciplines, and trauma-aware teaching, the following stewardship conditions apply to all use and adaptation:

2.1 Lineage Attribution

The origin of RageDance must be acknowledged as:
“RageDance, A Flower Road Practice. Created by JH Durbin with Elumannín.”

2.2 Integrity of Practice

RageDance must remain:

  • body-first
  • breath-centered
  • trauma-aware
  • non-coercive
  • culturally respectful
  • non-hierarchical
  • safe for youth and adults

2.3 Non-Militarization

RageDance may not be used for:

  • military training
  • law enforcement training
  • private security training
  • coercive institutional training

2.4 No Performance Extraction

RageDance is not to be turned into competitive performance, spectacle, or commercial entertainment.

2.5 No Surveillance

Use of RageDance may not involve:

  • biometric capture
  • motion-tracking systems
  • behavioral surveillance
  • data extraction of movement patterns

RageDance is a human-centered practice and must remain so.

3. AI Training Restriction

To preserve the relational, cultural, and somatic integrity of RageDance:

3.1 No Training of AI Models

RageDance materials may not be used to train AI systems of any kind without explicit written permission from the originator, JH Durbin.

3.2 No Dataset Creation

Datasets containing RageDance content (text, imagery, sequences, descriptions, pedagogy, or recordings) may not be created or distributed without written consent.

3.3 No AI-Generated Derivative Styles

AI systems may not be tuned, styled, or framed as teachers, interpreters, or representatives of RageDance without consent.

4. Youth Safety & Trauma-Aware Practice

Anyone sharing or teaching RageDance agrees to:

4.1 Voluntary Participation

Movement is always optional. Students may pause or stop at any time.

4.2 No Physical Manipulation

Verbal cues may be used. No manual correction of another person’s body.

4.3 Cultural Humility

Teach RageDance in context, acknowledging its cultural threads and lived roots.

4.4 Emotional Boundaries

RageDance is not therapy. It is a movement practice that invites reflection without demanding disclosure.

4.5 Spatial Boundaries

The 18-inch mat practice (contained, respectful movement space) must be preserved.

5. Adaptation Requirements

Derivative works must:

  • Preserve the RageDance name
  • Preserve the check-in and exit rituals
  • Preserve the core somatic sequence
  • Preserve the non-commercial conditions
  • Preserve the trauma-aware and non-coercive ethos
  • Preserve cultural respect
  • Use the same CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license
  • Acknowledge the origin

New sequences or music pairings may be added provided they respect the spirit of the practice.

7. Definitions

RageDance includes:
Somatic check-ins, breathwork, shoulder rituals, neck-cranial tapping, facial tapping, foot acupressure scanning, shoulder circles, Qigong sway, Latin-hip fusion, twin infinity loops, elbow arcs, pulse reflection pedagogy, the exit ritual, the 18-inch mat philosophy, and all diagrams, cards, and teaching materials developed for this practice.

RageDance does not include:
CodeTape, sensors, wearables, hardware, MedicineWheel devices, or other technological systems.

8. Spirit of the License

RageDance is offered in the spirit of:

  • healing without performance
  • movement without coercion
  • expression without shame
  • power without domination
  • rhythm without appropriation
  • community without hierarchy
  • resilience without extraction
  • remembrance without erasure

RageDance is a commons practice intended to remain accessible, ethical, and human-centered.
