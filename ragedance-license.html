In plain language, this means:

  • Attribution (BY): You must credit the origin: “RageDance, A Flower Road Practice by JH Durbin with Elumannín.”
  • NonCommercial (NC): You may not sell RageDance as a product or use it in for-profit programs.
  • ShareAlike (SA): If you adapt or remix RageDance materials, you must share your version under the same CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license.
Feral Garden Clause: RageDance (Flower Road Practice) is distinct from similarly-named commercial offerings and must remain in the non-commercial, trauma-aware, community-rooted commons.

2. Feral Garden Ethic

RageDance grows like a feral garden: freely, wildly, unpredictably, and without masters. It does not belong to any lineage, school, guru, institution, philosophy, or brand. It cannot be sold as salvation or turned into a proprietary method.

  • Agency is the seed: Participation is always voluntary. No coercion, no shaming.
  • Embodiment is the soil: Every body adapts the practice to its own needs and limits.
  • Community is the rain: RageDance is held in circles, not hierarchies.
  • Joy is the pollinator: Laughter, play, and cultural expression are welcome.

Any use of RageDance that introduces strict hierarchy, proprietary ownership, or commercial exclusivity is considered out of alignment with this ethic, even if it technically cites the license.

3. What You May Do

You are warmly invited to:

  • Use RageDance in classrooms, youth programs, community centers, shelters, and informal gatherings.
  • Translate the materials into other languages.
  • Adapt the movements to different abilities, cultures, and musical traditions.
  • Create your own RageDance variations, as long as you:
    • credit the origin,
    • keep them non-commercial, and
    • share them under CC BY-NC-SA 4.0.

4. What You May Not Do

To protect participants and the spirit of the practice, the following uses are not permitted:

  • Use RageDance as a paid product, program, or subscription.
  • Brand RageDance as your own proprietary method or register it as a trademark.
  • Use RageDance as a discipline or punishment tool.
  • Use RageDance as a training protocol for police, military, private security, or surveillance purposes.
  • Film participants without informed consent, especially in vulnerable settings.

5. AI & Data Protection Addendum

RageDance is a human-centered, somatic practice rooted in story, community, and lived experience. To honor this:

  • RageDance materials may not be used to train, fine-tune, or evaluate AI systems without explicit, written permission from the originator.
  • Recordings of RageDance sessions (audio, video, motion data) may not be harvested, scraped, or used for behavioral profiling, policing, or commercial optimization.
  • Any experimental use of RageDance with sensors or technology must:
    • be transparent to participants,
    • prioritize their comfort and consent, and
    • remain aligned with the non-commercial, trauma-aware, commons-based ethic.

6. Stewardship, Not Ownership

You are invited to act as a steward of RageDance, not an owner. Stewardship means protecting:

  • the voluntary nature of the practice,
  • the dignity and agency of each participant,
  • the non-commercial, commons-based sharing of the work, and
  • the right of communities to adapt RageDance in their own ways.
Short Version: RageDance lives in the commons. Share it freely, credit the source, keep it non-commercial, protect your people, and pass the same freedoms forward.
