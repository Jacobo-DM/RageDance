RD-001 · Full Cycle

Overview

This card gives you a single, complete RageDance session. It can stand alone or be used as a warm-up for longer practice.

~10–15 minutes
18-inch mat
No equipment
Trauma-aware

Sequence

  1. Arrival & Breath · 1 minute
    Stand in your square. Feet grounded, knees soft. Inhale through the nose, exhale through the mouth. On each exhale, let the shoulders drop a little.
  2. Body Check-In · 2 minutes
    Quietly scan: shoulders, chest, stomach, hips, legs, feet. Name what you notice: “tight”, “heavy”, “buzzing”, “numb”. No fixing, just listening.
  3. Neck & Facial Tapping · 1 minute
    Gently massage along the neck into the base of the skull. Tap lightly around the head and face with fingertips. Invite the jaw and eyes to soften.
  4. Shoulder Circles · 2 minutes
    Small circles forward, then backward. Gradually widen the circles, letting the chest open. Breath stays smooth; no rushing.
  5. Qigong Sway + Hips · 2 minutes
    Relaxed arm swinging, side-to-side weight shift. Add gentle hip motion (figure-8 if it feels natural). Let the spine move like a wave, not a rod.
  6. Twin Infinity Loops · 2 minutes
    Right and left arms draw looping patterns that cross the body and rise over the shoulders. Move slowly at first, then let the loops find a comfortable rhythm. Allow shoulders, spine, and hips to join without forcing it.
  7. Elbow Arc Drills · 2 minutes
    From center, lift the elbow through a controlled arc and return. Cover high, mid, and low diagonals. Train both sides equally; precision and breath matter more than speed.
  8. Pulse Reflection & Exit · 1 minute
    Return to stillness. Feel your heartbeat, feel your breath. Ask quietly: “What changed? What softened? What steadied?” End with one slow inhale and one long exhale.
Reminder: Participation is always voluntary. You can reduce repetitions, slow the tempo, or skip steps if your body says so. RageDance is a practice of listening, not forcing.

Suggested Use

Use RD-001 when you want a full reset:

  • before or after a hard conversation,
  • at the start or end of a school day,
  • between intense work blocks, or
  • anytime your pulse feels louder than your thoughts.
